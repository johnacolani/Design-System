## 1. Psychological Impact of Color

### Key Points:
- **Cultural Associations:** Colors have different meanings across cultures
  - White: Purity (Western) vs. Mourning (Eastern)
  - Need for cultural sensitivity research
- **Universal Psychological Meanings:**
  - Red: Energy, passion, excitement
  - Blue: Calmness, trust, reliability
- **Demographic Considerations:**
  - Men: Bold, vibrant colors (red, orange)
  - Women: Softer, pastel colors (pink, lavender)
  - Age: Younger = bright/bold, Older = muted/classic
- **Contrast Effects:**
  - High contrast: Vibrancy, excitement
  - Low contrast: Soothing, harmonious
- **Visual Harmony:**
  - Complementary colors (opposite on wheel)
  - Analogous colors (adjacent on wheel)

**Current Implementation:** ⚠️ Partial

## 2. Color Theory and Application

### Key Points:
- **Color Wheel Structure:**
  - Primary: Red, Blue, Yellow
  - Secondary: Green, Orange, Purple
  - Tertiary: Mixes of primary + secondary
- **Color Schemes:**
  - **Monochromatic:** Single color, shades/tints (clean, minimalist)
  - **Analogous:** Adjacent colors (harmonious, soothing)
  - **Complementary:** Opposite colors (bold, eye-catching)
  - **Triadic:** Three evenly spaced colors (vibrant, energetic)
- **Psychological Effects:**
  - Red: Passion, energy
  - Blue: Calm, trust
  - Yellow: Happiness, optimism
  - Green: Nature, growth
